Beginner's Guide to Paint and Body Work

Welcome to SorrowsGarage’s comprehensive beginner’s guide. This resource will walk you through the essentials of bodywork and automotive painting, from surface prep to finish coats.

1. Safety First

  • Always wear PPE – respirators, gloves, goggles, coveralls.
  • Ensure proper ventilation when sanding or painting.
  • Use a dust extraction system if possible.

2. Workspace Preparation

  • Clean, dry, and enclosed area with minimal dust.
  • Good lighting to catch imperfections.
  • Tools organized and within reach.

3. Understanding Body Fillers

Mix body filler with hardener in proper ratios. Spread thin layers and shape while it’s still soft using 80-grit sandpaper. Refine with 120 and 220 grit.

4. Sanding Guide

  • Start with 80–120 grit to shape body filler.
  • Use 180–220 grit for smoothing and feathering.
  • 320–400 grit for primer prep.
  • 600+ grit for final wet sanding before paint.

5. Priming the Surface

Use a high-build primer to fill small imperfections. Apply in thin, even coats. Block sand with 320–400 grit until surface is uniform and smooth.

6. Painting

  • Mix paint according to manufacturer’s recommendations.
  • Apply basecoat in light, even passes. 2–3 coats are typical.
  • Allow flash time between coats (usually 10–15 mins).

7. Clear Coating

  • Apply 2–3 coats of clear with consistent gun movement.
  • Maintain correct distance (usually 6–8 inches).
  • Let cure per spec before wet sanding or polishing.

8. Polishing and Finishing

  • Wet sand with 1500–3000 grit if needed.
  • Use compound and buffer to remove haze or texture.
  • Finish with polish and protectant.

9. Recommended Tools

  • DA Sander
  • Block sanders (flexible and rigid)
  • HVLP spray gun
  • Mixing cups, strainers, stir sticks
  • Tack cloths and masking materials

10. Practice & Patience

Get practice panels from a junkyard. Focus on one technique at a time and repeat. Quality comes from consistency and repetition.
